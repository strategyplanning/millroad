Our belief

We make one thing and we make it well: furniture you'll use every day and love every time.

We consider our work functional installation art — and we treat it that way, every time.

Given their size and their place in a home, a dining table, a bench, a countertop becomes a focal point. A quiet heirloom. Something you gather around. We take that seriously. Each commission begins with a conversation about the room it will live in and ends, months later, with a piece built to your specification — delivered and installed by the same hands that made it.

Wood, concrete, and epoxy — the three materials we've spent years learning to marry — let us build pieces that feel both ancient and modern. Warm and honest. Cool and precise. That's the Mill Road tension we're after.

The Craft

From a standing tree to a finished room.

Every commission moves through the same four-stage arc — sometimes over six weeks, sometimes over six months. You're welcome at any step, and most clients come by the shop at least once.

01
Selection

The slab & the sketch.

We meet, we measure the room, we pull a slab. Or we pour a form. The piece starts with a direction, not a drawing.

02
Design

Shaped to the space.

Proportions, height, base. You approve the scale and the joinery. Only then do we start cutting.

03
Build

By one pair of hands.

Hand-planed, mortised, finished. Concrete cures for weeks. Epoxy pours multiple times, demolded by hand.

04
Install

In your room, by us.

We deliver it. We carry it in. We install it, level it, and walk you through caring for it. Then we check in.

Our materials

Three natural mediums. One careful hand.

We've spent years learning how these three materials behave — how wood moves with the seasons, how concrete cures without cracking, how epoxy pours without bubbling. It's the combination that makes a Mill Road piece.

Wood

Walnut, white oak, maple, cherry — locally sourced where we can, live-edge wherever it's beautiful. Kiln-dried to 6–8% before it enters the shop.

Cast in our shop using a proprietary blend — stronger, lighter, thinner than standard concrete. Sealed against stains, heat, and a lifetime of family dinners.

Commonly asked

Questions we love answering twice.

Most dining tables run 10–16 weeks from contract to delivery. Smaller pieces (benches, coffee tables) run 6–10 weeks. Concrete countertops are paced around their 28-day cure. Rush timelines are possible — ask.

We deliver and install anywhere in Ontario ourselves. For the rest of Canada and the northern US, we crate pieces professionally and ship via specialty furniture freight. We've shipped to BC, Quebec, and New York without incident.

Yes — and most clients do at least once. We'll pick through slabs together, look at finish samples, and sit at a few tables to talk height and scale. The shop is just outside Guelph; visits are by appointment.

Every piece ships with written care instructions specific to its material. Oil-finished wood takes an occasional re-oil (we supply the first can). Sealed concrete is dishwasher-simple. Epoxy river tops clean up with a damp cloth. We're a phone call away for life.

Yes. Heritage wood — a tree from a family property, reclaimed beams from a barn — is some of our favourite material to work with. Tell us about it and we'll talk you through drying, milling, and whether the piece you're picturing is possible.
